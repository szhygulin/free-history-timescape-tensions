# Free-history timescape vs. the cosmological tensions

**Does a data-driven void history resolve not just the Hubble tension, but the full suite of ΛCDM tensions?**

Author: **Viacheslav Zhygulin**

Third in a series testing whether dark-energy-free inhomogeneous cosmology can replace ΛCDM:

1. [**timescape-hubble-tension**](https://github.com/szhygulin/timescape-hubble-tension) — standard (tracker) timescape does **not** resolve the Hubble tension; its one-parameter void history is too rigid to fit supernovae, BAO, and the CMB together.
2. [**free-history-timescape**](https://github.com/szhygulin/free-history-timescape) — freeing the void volume-fraction history `f_v(z)` from the tracker attractor (driving it from the observed void population, through the same Buchert averaging + clock-rate dressing) **does** reconcile the SN+BAO+CMB geometry, at a physically plausible present void fraction.
3. **this repo** — the decisive question: does free-history timescape resolve the **whole** Hubble tension end-to-end *and* the other cosmological conflicts at the same time, with one consistent model?

## The question

A geometric fit to distances (paper 2) is necessary but not sufficient. A cosmology that replaces ΛCDM must survive the *joint* set of tensions and precision tests, not just one. This paper asks whether free-history timescape does — across, at minimum:

- the **Hubble tension** (H₀), carried through to a self-consistent early+late determination rather than a distance-fit;
- the **S₈ / σ₈ structure-growth tension** (growth of structure, redshift-space distortions, weak lensing);
- the **CMB** beyond the acoustic scale (full acoustic peaks, ISW), **BBN** / early-Universe consistency, and the **sound-horizon** calibration;
- any new tensions the model itself introduces (e.g. a void population or expansion history in conflict with observations).
